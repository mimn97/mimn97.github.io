About

I am a second-year M.S. student in College of Information and Computer Sciences at University of Massachusetts Amherst. Previously, I received my B.A in Computer Science and Mathematics from The College of Wooster.

I'm applying for several PhD programs in Computer Science for Fall 2023! I am also looking for AI/ML residency programs or summer internships starting 2023!

My primary research interests lie in the intersection of data science and natural language processing (NLP). The long-term goal of my research is to develop statistical and AI tools for increasing public and population health and thus promote health equity. In particular, my research focuses on how to extract meaningful insights from different sources of unstructured data (e.g., clinical texts, social media posts, GPS information) using data science and NLP approaches.

Here are some keywords of my research interests, including but not limited to:

  • Data Science for Public Health (e.g., data-driven approaches to health disparity issues)
  • NLP for Public Health (e.g., developing large language models (LLMs) for pubilc health surveillance)
  • NLP for Social Media Analysis (e.g., processing noisy texts on social media platforms)
  • Equitable AI for Health (e.g., debiasing NLP models, clinical notes, or social media)
