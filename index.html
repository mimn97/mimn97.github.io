About

I recently graduated from M.S. program in Computer Science at University of Massachusetts Amherst. Previously, I received my double B.As in Computer Science and Mathematics from The College of Wooster.

My research goal is to develop AI-driven systems that tackle various societal challenges, such as promoting equitable access in healthcare, education, social welfare systems, and safer online communities. In my previous work, I utilized data mining and natural language processing (NLP) to (1) extract insights on social welfare disparities and (2) predict mental health risks in US neighborhoods from Twitter. I also contributed to research projects in deep learning and NLP, investigating clinical language models' performance in low-resource settings and enhancing vision-language models with Bayesian Inference and GPT-3. These previous experiences not only expanded our understanding of health and society but also demonstrated my versatility in different research domains.

I am eager to broaden my research interests to encompass fields like multimodal reasoning, causal inference, Human-centered AI and Human-Computer Interaction (HCI). By venturing into these areas, I aim to contribute towards making our societies more conducive to human well-being through advancements in AI.
